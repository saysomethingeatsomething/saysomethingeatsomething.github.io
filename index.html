Homework for US:

A Listening Project

We’ve been sitting in the back of class all semester, chatting with our friends. Now we have some make-up work to do: pop our bubbles and listen to people outside our own echo-chambers.

America, your assignment:

If you’re travelling for the holidays, go out into the community and listen to hopes and worries of strangers. How? We suggest food.

So here's the plan.

  1. Take an hour out of your holiday.
  2. Grab some candy, muffins, or warm apple cider.
  3. Stand on Main Street, outside the supermarket, or wherever people are.
  4. Offer a stranger something to eat in exchange for hearing what they’re hoping for in their communities right now.
  5. Listen and empathize. Remember, we are not here to argue. This is not a political discussion.

Ready? Let's do it.

Commit to finish your homework, and assign it to a friend too.

Be prepared.

  • Conversations could veer into uncomfortable territory. Listen, don't try to argue or escalate. Our goal is to understand feelings, not change them.
  • Protect yourself. Work in pairs, in a public place.
