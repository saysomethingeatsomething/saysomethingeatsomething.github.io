Homework for US,

a listening project.

We’ve been sitting in the back of class all semester, chatting with our friends. Now we have some make-up work to do: pop our bubbles and listen to people outside our own echo-chambers.

Get ready.

Wear a t-shirt or display a sign to initiate and introduce yourself to passerby. We have a t-shirt that you can buy (we’re selling it at cost), and PDFs that you can print.

How you choose to do this is up to you, but it should be legible at a distance, approachable, and explain our goal clearly.

Download posters.
Order a shirt.

Be prepared.

  • Conversations could veer into uncomfortable territory.
  • Expect to hear things that you don't personally agree with.
  • Listen, don't try to argue or escalate. Our goal is to understand feelings, not change them.
  • Ask thoughtful followup questions. Let people say as much as they want.
  • Protect yourself. Work in pairs, in a public place.

Share what you've heard.

Have a good conversation? Let the world know! Tweet or post on Instagram with #hw4us, but be sure not to violate the privacy of anyone you've met.

Notes.

We recognize that not everyone has the ability to travel for the holidays. We believe that those of us that are traveling have a responsibility to use this economic privilege to help understand, share, and bridge divides. Similarly, not everyone has the privilege to talk to strangers without fear of harassment.

If you are unable to offer "food for your thoughts" this winter, consider asking others who can. Share your own hopes, or the hopes of your local community, with #hw4us.

Ready? Let's do it.

Commit to finish your homework, and assign it to a friend too.
